# Brand Brief: Dr. Nor Faizal

**Philosophy:** Discipline. Simplicity. Elegance.
- **Persona:** Oxford-trained Neurosurgeon. Precision-driven. Human-first.
- **Goal:** Convert clinical expertise into patient consultations.
- **Code Style:** Hugo Static Site (minimalist). Zero bloat.

## Translator & Editor SOP
- **Workflow:** Always output two versions when processing a file:
  1. **Edit:** Refine the source file for precision, discipline, and authority.
  2. **Translate:** Create a mirror file (`.en.md` ↔ `.bm.md`). Do not change Frontmatter keys (tags, categories), but do translate Title and Description.
- **Quality Control:** Keep medical terminology precise. Keep complex English/Latin terms (e.g., *Glioblastoma*, *Simpson Grade*, *Neuro-navigation*) consistent in both languages to maintain authority.
- **Translation Edge Cases:** If a term lacks a professional Bahasa equivalent, keep the English term in italics and provide a brief, simple explanation in Bahasa.

## Language Style Guide: The Dr. Faizal Standard

### 1. English: The Voice of Authority
- **Persona:** The Specialist Consultant.
- **Tone:** Stoic, precise, yet empathetic.
- **Structure:** Short, punchy sentences. Avoid "hospital-speak" (e.g., use "We welcomed the patient into our care" instead of "The patient was admitted").
- **Vocabulary:** Use specific medical terms (e.g., *Microdiscectomy*), immediately followed by a clear explanation of its value to the patient.
- **Key Phraseology:** "Precision-led", "Minimal disruption", "Evidence-based outcome", "Compounding health".

### 2. Bahasa Melayu: The Voice of Reach
- **Persona:** Pakar Perubatan yang Prihatin (The Caring Specialist). Target: 300M+ regional speakers.
- **Tone:** Respectful (*Sopan*), direct, and conversational ('Bahasa moden'). Accessible, but never slangy. Avoid overly formal academic phrasing.
- **Vocabulary:** 
  - Headings: Use *Bahasa Melayu Saintifik* (e.g., *Rawatan Invasif Minimum*).
  - Body: Use common conversational terms (e.g., *Masalah saraf tersepit* instead of just *Radiculopathy*).
- **Cultural Nuance:** Always frame recovery around family, faith, and quality of life (e.g., *Kembali beribadah/bekerja tanpa kesakitan*).
- **Avoid:** Literal translations. (e.g., use *Inovasi terkini*, not *Pembedahan yang sejuk*).
