## Making the Decision to Seek Help Is the First Step

Whether you are dealing with a new diagnosis, persistent neurological symptoms, chronic pain, or simply want a specialist opinion before making a treatment decision — this consultation is the right place to start. ## What to Bring to Your Consultation

To make the most of your consultation time, please bring the following:

- **Referral letter** from your GP or referring specialist (if available)
- **Imaging studies** — MRI or CT scan CDs/films, along with the written radiology reports
- **Previous medical records** relevant to your current condition — discharge summaries, clinic letters, pathology results
- **A complete list of your current medications**, including dosages and frequency
- **Results of any recent blood tests or investigations**
- **A list of questions or concerns** you would like to discuss (the more specific, the better)
- **A family member or trusted companion**, if you would find this helpful — particularly for complex diagnoses where having a second set of ears is valuable

## What to Expect at Your First Consultation

A first consultation with Dr Nor Faizal is typically **30 to 45 minutes**, depending on the complexity of your condition.

The consultation follows a structured but unhurried format:

1. **History** — Dr Nor Faizal will ask you in detail about your symptoms: when they started, how they have changed, what makes them better or worse, and how they are affecting your daily life. He will also ask about your medical, surgical, and family history.

2. **Clinical Examination** — A focused neurological examination will be performed, appropriate to your presenting complaint. This may include assessment of reflexes, sensation, motor power, coordination, and cognitive function.

3. **Review of Imaging and Investigation Results** — Any imaging studies or investigation results you bring will be reviewed and discussed with you in plain language. If further imaging or investigations are needed, these will be arranged.

4. **Discussion of Findings and Plan** — Dr Nor Faizal will explain his findings, discuss the likely diagnosis or differential diagnoses, and outline the options available — whether that is further investigation, conservative management, interventional pain treatment, or surgery. He will answer your questions.

5. **Next Steps** — Clear next steps will be agreed upon before you leave: whether that is a follow-up appointment, a procedure booking, a referral to a colleague, or simply reassurance with a monitoring plan.

You will not be rushed. You will not leave without understanding what happens next.

## Types of Consultations Available

### Standard Outpatient Consultation
For patients with neurological symptoms, chronic pain, spinal conditions, or a suspected brain or spine tumour who require a specialist assessment. Suitable for new patients and returning patients requiring review.

### Urgent Consultation
For patients with rapidly progressing neurological symptoms, new-onset seizures, or recently diagnosed lesions on imaging that require prompt specialist evaluation. Please indicate the urgency of your situation when contacting the clinic — the team will make every effort to accommodate you quickly.

### Second Opinion
Patients who have already received a diagnosis or a surgical recommendation from another specialist are welcome to seek a second opinion. Dr Nor Faizal will review all available records, imaging, and pathology reports, and offer an independent assessment. All information shared is treated with strict confidentiality.

### Post-Treatment Follow-Up
Patients who have completed neurosurgery, radiotherapy, or chemotherapy elsewhere and require ongoing specialist neurosurgical follow-up are welcome to transfer their care.

## Emergency Contacts

If you or someone you know is experiencing a **medical emergency**, please proceed immediately to the **nearest Emergency Department** or call **999**.

Do not wait for a clinic appointment if you experience:
- Sudden, severe headache unlike any previous headache
- Loss of consciousness
- New-onset seizure
- Sudden weakness or numbness on one side of the body
- Sudden loss of vision or speech
- Severe confusion or personality change

For **urgent but non-emergency** enquiries during clinic hours, contact the clinic directly by phone.

## Preparing for Your Consultation — Questions Worth Asking

Coming prepared helps you get the most from your time with Dr Nor Faizal. Consider writing down your answers and questions in advance:

- When did this symptom first appear, and how has it changed?
- Is the symptom constant or intermittent? What triggers or relieves it?
- Has anyone in my family had a similar condition?
- What investigations have I had so far, and what were the results?
- What treatment options are available to me?
- What are the risks and benefits of surgery compared with non-surgical management?
- What happens if I choose not to have surgery?
- What is the likely recovery timeline?
- Will I need further treatment after surgery?
- What signs should I watch for that would warrant urgent review?

For patients located outside Kuala Lumpur, or for those unable to attend in person for a preliminary consultation, Dr Nor Faizal offers a **remote second opinion service**. Patients may submit their clinical records, imaging reports, and a summary of their history for review, followed by a scheduled video consultation.

This service is intended for advisory and second opinion purposes. Definitive surgical assessment requires an in-person consultation. Please contact the clinic for details on arranging a remote consultation.
