## Behind the Scrubs — Neurosurgery Up Close

Many patients wonder what happens between booking an appointment and going home after surgery. This Instagram post offers a glimpse into the day-to-day rhythm of a neurosurgical practice.

## What you see in the post

From pre-operative planning to post-operative care, neurosurgery is a discipline built on meticulous preparation and teamwork. A few things the post illustrates:

### Pre-operative planning
Every case starts long before the patient enters the theatre. Reviewing MRI and CT scans, mapping tumour boundaries, and planning the surgical corridor takes hours — sometimes days.

### The operating theatre team
A neurosurgical procedure involves a neurosurgeon, anaesthesiologist, scrub nurse, circulating nurse, and often a neurophysiology monitoring team. The coordination is precise.

### Intraoperative monitoring
For tumours near eloquent brain areas (speech, movement, vision), Dr Nor Faizal uses intraoperative neurophysiology monitoring to protect these critical functions during surgery.

### Recovery and rehabilitation
Surgery is one chapter. Recovery — physiotherapy, speech therapy, occupational therapy — is the next. The goal is always function, not just tumour removal.
